02

Rules the UI Obeys

every screen is measured against these

Story first

Text is the art. Chrome serves the words, then disappears. Typography carries the mood before any color does.

One decision at a time

The screen centers the current situation and its choices — never a dashboard of competing demands. Depth is one tap away.

Color communicates

The base is desaturated. Saturated hue is a signal, not decoration: ember=act, blood=danger, bile=infection, teal=relief.

Minimal status

Only critical stats are ever visible. Infection is never a bar — it surfaces as a symptom tag and as degraded text.

Show real costs

Choices display known costs (time, noise, a resource) and hide the outcome. Never a fake choice.

Restraint is a tool

In loss, the UI strips back — the Quiet Screen. It can go dark when the power fails and degrade when the player is feverish.

Anatomy — top to bottom
  1. Header — system voice. Day, time, phase, weather in mono. This is the world talking, not the story. Never competes with the prose.
  2. Location strip. Answers “where am I?” Region muted, node bright. Tapping opens the map.
  3. Status — critical only. HP red at 24% (blood token). Infection is not a bar — it shows as the FEVERISH tag and, deeper in, by degrading this very screen.
  4. Story window — the star. Largest type, most breathing room. Ember marks an interactable noun (locked cabinet); blood marks a threat (stockroom).
  5. Micro-choices. Cheap, tactile taps in steel/info. They rarely swing a run alone but compound — the door you didn’t close.
  6. Choice rows. Known costs shown in mono (LOUD · 10m); outcome always hidden. The 3px ember left-edge = “this is interactive.” Ghost row = low-commitment exit.
  7. Bottom nav — depth on demand. Map · Pack · People · Radio · Journal. One tap away, never crowding the decision.

What changes in combat
  1. The frame reddens, not reddens-everything. Header contact dot, threat pill, and threat nouns take the blood token. The base stays charcoal so danger still reads as danger.
  2. Actions replace exploration choices. Attack · Push · Fire · Flee. Each shows its scarce cost — stamina, or NOISE +12 for the gun, the loudest and most dangerous “win.”
  3. Escape is always first-class. The teal “Break for the door” is never hidden. The best fight is the one avoided.
  4. Fear is felt, not metered. As the hidden Fear rises, prose shortens and blurs and options quietly thin. No fear bar — the writing carries it.
  5. Nav swaps to combat verbs. Fight · Pack (quick-swap weapon) · Guard. Depth still one tap away, tuned to the moment.

Pack rules
  1. Weight is the pressure. The carry bar warns amber past 70%. Over-capacity forces an explicit drop/replace — never a silent “can’t pick up.”
  2. Categories, not clutter. Filter chips keep the list to one intent at a time. Ember chip = active filter.
  3. Artifacts carry history. “Sarah’s flashlight — found in the hospital, repaired twice.” The teal ARTIFACT tag means it has provenance and losing it will hurt.
  4. The last can is visible. Copy foreshadows the moral squeeze (“someone will ask”). The economy is designed to make food a choice.
  5. No XP anywhere. Growth is this list — a better axe, a working light, a full pack. There is no level to see.

People, not spawns
  1. Trust is per-relationship. Each person shows their own trust (teal when high, plain when guarded) — no single global reputation bar.

  5. Everyone is a mouth and a risk. Three with you, five at home — the header keeps the cost of the community present.

The shelter is a character
  1. One home, evolving. Built rooms glow; unbuilt ones are dashed “+ build” slots. The shelter grows from a barricaded room into a community.
  2. It lives while you’re away. The steel-blue Daily Report is how the simulation tells you what your absence cost — arguments, weather, the fever that held.
  3. Hope vs. survival. Morale sits amber at 41. Cold-optimal calls (quarantine Eli, ration hard) keep people alive but bleed morale — and a shelter without hope falls from inside.
  4. Night is the bill. “Sleep” carries a blood-red RISK: NIGHT — attacks land on the preparation of the days before.
  5. Food at 2 days. Critical stat in red. The countdown is the pressure that pushes you back outside.

The map is the story
  1. Fog of war. Only 41% known. Dashed “?” nodes are rumor and blank space — discovery is its own reward.
  2. Regions carry threat. Downtown glows faint blood-red at “HIGH.” The wash and label read the region’s evolving danger before you travel.
  3. Home and here. Teal marks the safe Overlook; the pulsing ember “YOU” is the current node. Color instantly separates safe from active.
  4. Nodes remember. The pharmacy stays outlined blood-red because of what happened there — the map holds the memory, not just the geography.
  5. Your handwriting. Player notes pinned in a script face — “lost Marcus here?”, “good water ↓.” By the end, the map is a personal archive.
  6. Travel has a price. Every route shows time and noise up front — a Survival-Triangle trade, never a free move.

Never explain everything
  1. Three layers, your pace. Your story, the city’s story, and the truth. Fragments — not chapters — accumulate into a codex.

Restraint as design
  1. Everything falls away. No stats, no nav, no choices but one. Whitespace and a single ember hairline give the moment room to land.
  2. A death you can name. The last line states the decision that killed you — “you held the door.” The Last Stand made it a scene.
  3. No “You Died.” The one action leads to an epilogue and run summary — the story continues past the character.
  4. Same device for grief mid-run. After any major loss, the game can go this quiet for a beat, then return.
06

States, Motion & Access

how the system flexes

Feverish / infected

As infection advances, story text desaturates, letter-spacing drifts, and a word may be “wrong.” The bile tag is the only overt tell — the rest is felt.

Power out

When the grid fails, the whole UI dims a step, the header loses color, and night screens go darker. The interface tells the world’s story too.

Motion

Minimal and diegetic. Story text arrives with weight; a failing light flickers; nothing bounces. Transitions are cross-fades, not slides.

Focus & touch

Focus ring is a 2px ember halo. Targets ≥ 44px, primary actions in the lower third for one-hand reach.

Accessibility

Color is never the sole signal — every hue pairs with a label or icon. Scales to 200% text, ships a high-contrast mode, and reads cleanly to a screen reader (it’s mostly text).

One system, many clients

These tokens and components are the source of truth for web, native, and a future chat-bot client. Same palette everywhere.
