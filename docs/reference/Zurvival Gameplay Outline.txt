Zurvival (Original Kik Bot) – Pure Gameplay Outline
(Single‑player focus, no chat/social features)

1. Core Loop
Game runs in turns.

Each turn you choose one main action.

The game resolves that action, updates your stats, describes what happens, then advances time.

2. Starting the Game
Begin a new run.

You start with:

Basic health.

A weak starting weapon (often melee).

Minimal or no supplies.

A starting location on the map.

3. Player Stats
Core tracked values:

Health (HP)

Damage from zombies reduces HP.

HP reaching 0 = death / run ends.

Stamina (used in many versions)

Spent on actions like running, melee attacks, heavy searches.

Regenerates over time or when resting.

Ammo (for ranged weapons)

Consumed when shooting.

Inventory

Weapons, ammo, meds, food, etc.

Limited by slots or weight (depending on implementation).

Optional stats some versions used:

Hunger / Thirst.

Infection level.

4. Map & Locations
World is a set of locations connected to each other.

Typical locations:

Houses, streets, stores, police station, hospital, mall, etc.

Each location has:

A description.

Possible loot.

A chance to contain or spawn zombies.

Connections to other locations you can move to.

You can usually:

Look / Map – see current location and exits.

Move – go to a connected location.

5. Available Actions (Per Turn)
You generally get one primary action per turn:

Move
Go to an adjacent/connected location.

Advances time.

May trigger an encounter on arrival.

Search / Scavenge
Search the current location for items.

Consumes time and often stamina.

Rolls against a loot table to give you:

Weapons

Ammo

Medkits / healing items

Food / drink

Occasionally special or story items

Can trigger zombies or an ambush.

Attack / Fight
Used when zombies are present.

Choose to:

Melee attack (uses stamina, no ammo).

Shoot (uses ammo, may attract more zombies).

Sometimes flee to another location.

Game resolves:

Hit/miss.

Damage to zombies.

Damage to you from counterattacks.

Combat may:

Be resolved in one summary per turn, or

Run as a short sub‑sequence of turns until enemies are dead, you flee, or you die.

Rest / Heal
Rest:

Takes time.

Restores stamina, sometimes HP.

May increase risk of zombie encounters unless in a safe spot.

Use Medkit / Heal:

Consumes a healing item.

Restores HP.

May take a turn or be part of resting.

Inventory Management
View inventory.

Equip weapon (set active melee or ranged).

Drop item.

Consume item (food, drink, meds).

(If crafting exists:) Craft using materials.

6. Zombies & Encounters
Zombies appear:

When you enter certain locations.

When you search.

As scheduled waves or end‑of‑turn spawns.

Base behavior:

Attack you each turn they’re present.

Deal damage that reduces your HP.

Stronger variants may exist:

Faster, tougher, or higher damage enemies.

Special zombies with unique effects (e.g., infection).

Spawn rates often increase with:

Time survived / day count.

Noisy actions (e.g., gunfire).

Higher‑risk locations.

7. Items & Equipment
Weapons
Melee: bat, knife, crowbar, etc.

No ammo.

Uses stamina.

Generally silent.

Ranged: pistol, shotgun, rifle, etc.

Requires matching ammo.

Higher damage, but can attract more zombies.

Each weapon effectively defines:

Damage potential.

Accuracy / hit chance.

Resource cost (stamina or ammo).

Ammo
Separate inventory item.

Required for ranged weapons.

Consumed per shot.

Healing & Consumables
Medkits / bandages – restore HP.

Food / water – restore hunger/thirst (if present), sometimes small HP/stamina benefits.

Optional buffs (stims, etc.).

Other Items
Crafting materials (if crafting is included).

Key/story items to unlock areas or trigger endgame.

8. Turn Resolution Flow (Abstract)
Each turn:

Show current:

HP, stamina, ammo, inventory summary.

Location and description.

Visible threats (zombies, hazards).

Player chooses one action (move, search, attack, rest, etc.).

Game resolves:

Movement and new location, or

Loot roll and item gain, or

Combat outcome, or

Rest/heal effects.

Apply passive changes:

Time advance.

Stamina/hunger/thirst changes.

Status effects (injury, infection, etc.).

Spawn or update zombies as needed.

Check for:

Death (HP ≤ 0) → run ends.

Win condition (if any) → endgame.

9. Win / Loss Conditions
Loss
Death:

HP drops to 0.

Run ends; game shows stats (days survived, zombies killed, etc.).

Win (varies by version/mode)
Possible end conditions:

Survive a set number of days/turns.

Reach a specific “safe zone” or extraction point.

Complete objectives (collect key items, visit certain locations).

Survive a final “invasion” event at a designated location.

If no explicit win condition is defined, the game is endless survival, with score based on how long you last and what you accomplish before dying.

This is the distilled gameplay outline of the original Zurvival / Zombie Invasion bot, stripped of all chat and social elements and presented as pure single‑player mechanics.
