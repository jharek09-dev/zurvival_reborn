# QA Review — M4 Part 6 (T50): The radio network

**Scope:** T50 — FR-STY-03, the radio network: emergency / military / civilian / ham / unknown signals that **evolve with world state**, heard on a scavenged receiver; listening cheap, broadcasting a loud unknown-audience risk (touching FR-STY-04); one reserved anomaly. GDD Part XIII "The radio network"; the M4 wireframe **SCR-09**.
**Build verified (clean sandbox):** engine **471** · content-loader **9** · harness **93** · typecheck clean ×3 · schema gate **135 entries / 10 types** · malformed rejected · empty-turn smoke exit 0.
**Save schema:** **no rung** — stays `v9`. Nothing is added to the state *shape*: a signal's status/message is **derived**, and what the player accumulates rides `history` (append-only `radio.tuned` / `radio.broadcast` beats), `NodeState.noise` (a broadcast), the open `rng.streams` map (a lazily seeded `radio` stream, drawn only by a broadcast), and inventory (`item.radio`).
**Owner call worth flagging:** signals are **derived, not stored**, so the network evolves for free as the world drifts — no radio sim-layer, the 14-stage pipeline untouched. The trade is that the network's evolution is bounded by what the *world sim reachably produces* (region threat mostly decays today; the horde→threat coupling that makes districts actively climb/fall is deferred — see PL-M4-26). The shipped network evolves reachably regardless (grid/lifespan death, phase, director escalation to *failing*, already-fallen districts as dead-air rows, and two latches), and the anomaly is reachable-but-rare.

## What shipped

A new `sim/radio.ts` interprets `content/radio/*.json` generically (the T47 encounter idiom — no per-signal branching), over a transient `graph.signals` pool threaded like the node graph. The whole system is **gated on carrying `item.radio`** (a scavenged receiver) and inert otherwise, so every prior run is byte-identical.

- **FR-STY-03 — five families, evolving with world state.** `signalStatus` is a pure function of world + region + day: **emergency** dies when the grid drops below `POWER_DEAD_AT` or its `lifespanDays` shelf life elapses; **military** reads live → failing → dead against its region's threat (`MILITARY_FAILING_AT 45` / `REGION_FALL_AT 65` — the already-overrun Downtown checkpoint is a **dead-air row from the start**, the Hillcrest evac holds); **civilian/ham** fall silent when their region is overrun (`REGION_SILENT_AT 60`); the **unknown** number station is faint by day and clear at night. Signals **age** (from `onsetDay`) and a lost one **decays to a dead-air row that stays listed** — the silence is content (SCR-09).
- **Two append-only latches** keep the derived model honest against a live world (both ride the `radio.tuned` beats, no stored state): a **discovered** signal stays on the dial (so an onset signal can't blink with the day/night tide, and a dead-air row persists), and a station heard **dead stays dead** (a fallen station never un-falls even if its district later calms).
- **Listen** (`LISTEN_COST 1h`, cheap) surfaces the on-air digest — rendered from the turn's `radio.tuned` beat, so a within-turn world drift can't change what you heard. **Broadcast** (`BROADCAST_COST 1h`) is loud: `params.noise = BROADCAST_NOISE 70` (via the T14 override, like a firearm) deposits region-scale noise at your node → the T26 dead re-path to you. The audience is the honest unknown — one seeded draw on the lazy `radio` stream picks a "who hears?" hint (never a spawn); the loudness is stated pre-commit (the choice label) and post-hoc ("it carries far, too far") regardless of outcome.
- **The reserved anomaly** breaks the row format exactly once (bare — no channel, no pill, no age, unquoted narration; nothing else ever departs from the format), gated to a **reachable-but-rare** deep-crisis: a deadly district (`≥ ANOMALY_THREAT 60`) or a blackout (`grid ≤ 25`), at night, once per run.
- **Content — a new schema-validated type.** `content/schemas/radio.schema.json` + seven signals (all five families + the anomaly) take the gate **9 → 10 types**. `world.broadcasts` stays reserved (the derived model doesn't populate it).

## Verification

- **Every prior golden is byte-identical.** The system is inert without a signals pool *and* without `item.radio`. Critically, `item.radio` is appended to the loot tables **only when the radio system is active** (a pool is registered) — a `floor(f·len)` pick shifts every index when a table grows, so mutating the shared tables would silently change loot draws in radio-less runs; gating it on the pool keeps them drawing exactly as before. The engine baseline that was 449 stayed green; the 449→471 growth is all new tests; harness 85→93 likewise.
- **Determinism.** `signalStatus`/`signalAudible`/`audibleSignals`/`readSignal`/`radioLine`/`renderSignalRead` advance no rng — a render (`sceneOf → radioLine`) is pure and resume-safe. A **broadcast** is the sole rng consumer, drawing exactly once from the `radio` stream (same seed ⇒ same outcome; every other stream byte-identical); a **listen** takes no draw.
- **The anomaly reveal is drift-proof.** The digest is rendered from the *logged* `radio.tuned` beat, not a re-derivation, so an anomaly caught at stage 3 survives the region-drift that runs later in the same turn (proven end-to-end: threat drifts below the gate within the turn, yet the anomaly is still in the scene). The once-per-run gate reads only *prior* turns' beats, so the reveal turn shows it and every later turn suppresses it.
- **Reachability (not just gate logic).** The anomaly test drives a **shipped** state — standing in the Mercy-Hospital district (baseline threat 80) at night — no hand-forced dials; the emergency loop reaches dead air at a day count a run can survive to. Both prove the network evolves in real play, not only under synthetic inputs.
- **Legibility (NFR-ACC-01 / FR-UI-02).** The digest is all words: a family tag, a channel number (allowed), a `LIVE`/`FAINT`/`DEAD AIR` word (never a bar), authored prose, and a plain-words age. No engine dial — region threat, `powerGrid`, `globalThreat`, infection progression — ever surfaces. Screen-reader-safe.
- **No no-op resolved turn.** A listen always logs a beat (history) and drifts needs; a broadcast changes rng + history + node noise + needs. Neither can be a no-consequence turn (FR-CORE-04).
- **Save-losslessness.** A run round-trips deep-equal across a broadcast (the lazy `radio` stream serializes). No schema rung; old saves load and behave identically.

## Adversarial audit → fixes applied

Two subagents reviewed in parallel — an engineering lens (determinism / save / byte-identity / edge cases) and a design/comprehension lens (legibility / no-number leak / FR-STY-03 fidelity / broadcast-risk fairness / voice). Both confirmed the module's purity, save discipline, reach model, and prose voice as **sound**. Fixes applied:

- **HIGH (eng) — loot byte-identity.** Appending `item.radio` to the shared loot tables shifted item selection for *radio-less* runs (a `floor(f·len)` pick), silently breaking their byte-identity (the behavioral suite couldn't catch it — no frozen goldens). Reverted the tables; the radio is now appended **only when the radio system is active**, threaded from the pool. Added the missing `ITEM_WEIGHTS` entry.
- **HIGH (eng) — the anomaly reveal was eaten by drift.** The reveal was logged at stage 3 but the digest re-derived audibility at stage 14, so a 1-point region drift within the turn could drop the anomaly below its gate *after* it was consumed once-per-run — the player never saw it. `radioLine` now renders the **logged** signal set (pinning what was on air at tune time); a new end-to-end test proves the anomaly survives the within-turn drift.
- **HIGH (design) — the network's evolution was unreachable** with the shipped baselines: region threat mostly *decays*, so `ANOMALY_THREAT = 85` could never be met and "a station goes dark" never animated. Retuned every threshold to the band the sim actually produces, made the anomaly gate reachable-but-rare (deadly district / blackout, at night), and added the two latches so evolution is one-way and permanent. The reachability gap that remains (a district *actively climbing* to a fall) is honestly parked on the deferred horde→threat coupling (PL-M4-26).
- **HIGH (design) — broadcast risk was not legible pre-commit.** The choice showed only a time cost; its one real consequence (it's loud, and draws the dead) was invisible. The label now states "loud; who hears is unknown," and the outcome line states "it carries far, too far" regardless of which seeded reply lands, so "nothing answers" never reads as free.
- **MED — onset signals blinked with the tide** (now latched listed via `everHeard`); **MED — the digest repeated the family/channel** in both the prefix and the label (labels rewritten to the *who* only); **MED — a civilian dead line hardcoded "the fourth day"** the derived model can't know (made day-agnostic); **LOW — the anomaly** was quoted and given a normal `LIVE` pill (now rendered bare); **LOW — `strengthOf` took an unused `graph`** (removed).

## Parking lot / deferrals

- **PL-M4-24 — the rumor system (FR-STY-05, Could/v1):** the ham/civilian leads are honest prose only; turning them into *variably-reliable, resolvable* leads (loot / location / trap) is deferred.
- **PL-M4-25 — deeper broadcasting (FR-STY-04, Could/v1):** the "who hears?" is a seeded hint; a real survivor/faction *reply* needs the people-side off-screen sim (PL-M3-02) + factions (T53). A radio-room (T52) would let you broadcast without lighting up your own node, and widen listening reach (hear locals citywide).
- **PL-M4-26 — the live "district falls" dynamic:** the status model reflects live world state, but region threat mostly *decays* in the current sim (no upward driver — the horde→threat coupling is deferred, PL-M2-05), so a district *climbing* to `REGION_FALL_AT` while you watch isn't reachable today; the shipped network evolves via grid/lifespan death, phase, the director escalating a camped region to *failing*, already-fallen high-baseline districts (dead-air rows), and the two latches. Land the live threat-climb fall with the deferred coupling.
- **PL-M4-27 — first-pass dials + content, and the voice pass:** all thresholds/costs/`BROADCAST_NOISE`/`item.radio` weight/EAS lifespan are untuned vs a real cross-city run (M5 T59/T60); the seven-signal set is a demonstrator across all families, not the launch pool; the owner's voice/casting pass on the signal prose is owed (as for characters/encounters, PL-M4-12). `onsetThreat` ("a new signal after a global event") is supported + unit-tested but not in the shipped demonstrator — the anomaly ships that concept.
- **PL-M4-28 — radio depth surfaces + perf:** `world.broadcasts` stays reserved (a future cache or the T54 Journal could populate it); the `radio.tuned` beats are the raw material for the T54 codex that "quietly assembles the fragments"; the `everHeard`/`everHeardDead` latches scan history per read (early-exit-bounded, fine at Rivermouth scale — a very long run's unbounded history, PL-M2-06, could want a cap).
