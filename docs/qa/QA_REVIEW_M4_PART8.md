# QA Review — M4 Part 8 (T52): Shelter depth — jobs & craftable rooms

**Scope:** T52 — FR-SHL-03 (assignable jobs that run while the player is away, producing/consuming the shared stash) and FR-SHL-04 (craftable rooms that unlock capability). GDD **Part XI** ("Shelter, Rooms & Jobs" — the base as a second loop: residents cooking, foraging, building). Also closes the T51 deferrals **PL-M3-01** (keeping people a base loop, not a pack drain), **PL-M3-05** (off-screen shelter upkeep), and **PL-M4-29** (the community fridge + a fuel-burning generator), and lands the off-screen half of **PL-M4-08** (companion orders tick off-screen) and **PL-M4-25** (a radio room shields a broadcast).
**Build verified (clean cloud sandbox):** engine **533** · content-loader **9** · harness **106** · typecheck clean ×3 · schema gate **157 entries / 12 types** · malformed rejected · empty-turn smoke exit 0.
**Save schema:** **NO rung — stays v10.** Job assignment is stored the T45 way — a `job:<id>` flag on the `Survivor` — so, like T45/T50, T52 touches no migration code. Everything a job *moves* rides shapes that already exist (`player.stash`, `world.powerGrid`, `NodeState.barricades`, companion `needs`); rooms already persist (`NodeState.rooms`, v10); stash spoilage is rate-based (no clock). Save-lossless by inspection.
**Owner call worth flagging:** one content-driven system makes the base self-running rather than four bolted-on base widgets — a garden banks fresh food, a kitchen preserves it and refrigerates the cache, the generator burns the thinnest resource (fuel) to hold the grid and keep the fridge cold, and the cache feeds the residents off the stash. The whole system is **dark without a job pool**, so every prior run is byte-identical (proven cross-tree against the pre-T52 baseline, below).

## What shipped

A new `sim/jobs.ts` interprets `content/jobs/*.json` generically (the T47/T50/T51 idiom — no per-job branching), over a transient `graph.jobs` pool threaded like the recipe pool. The seam mirrors economy's `Choices / isAction / resolve / Line`, wired into the same sites in `coreActions.ts`. The whole system is **gated on an active job pool** and (for assignment) on standing in your own shelter with a room built and a companion present, so it's inert otherwise.

- **FR-SHL-03 — assignable jobs that run on your turns and off-screen.** Assign a resident companion (a free, 0-hour `assign-job` verb, like the T45 orders / T39 stash) to a room's job; `tickShelterOps` runs it in pipeline stage 5 (beside `tickCompanions`) **and** in `advanceWorld`, so a played hour and a fast-forwarded hour do the same thing. Six jobs demonstrate all four shapes: **produce-only** (garden → `item.food-fresh`), **consume→produce** (kitchen fresh→canned; infirmary cloth→bandage; salvage → scrap), **hold-power** (generator burns `item.fuel` to raise `world.powerGrid`), and **upkeep** (watch keeps the shelter's barricades up). A cycle is `trunc(hours / hoursPerCycle)`; a job short of its input **stalls** (never drives the stash negative), and a hold-power/upkeep job at a full grid/wall stalls **before** burning its input (no wasted fuel).
- **FR-SHL-04 — craftable rooms that unlock capability.** Five new shelter recipes (garden/kitchen/watchtower/generator/radio) reuse the T51 `recipe.schema.json` (`installsRoom`) — **no new content type for rooms**. Every workable room now *does* something: workshop → crafting (T51) + `job.salvage`; medical → crafting (T51) + `job.infirmary`; garden → `job.garden`; kitchen → `job.kitchen` **+ the fridge** (stash fresh food doesn't spoil while it stands); watchtower → **halves off-screen barricade decay** (a lookout) **+** `job.watch`; generator → `job.generator`; radio → **broadcast from the shelter no longer lights up your node** (PL-M4-25).
- **Closes PL-M3-01 — the base feeds its people.** Each tick, a resident whose hunger/thirst is pressing is fed from the stash (fresh food first, then cans) — so a stocked base keeps people alive without touching the pack. Feeding runs **before** the drift/death pass, so a base with food in the cache doesn't lose someone a beat before it feeds them.
- **Closes PL-M3-05 / PL-M4-29 — the base runs while you're gone.** `advanceWorld` (gated on `jobsActive`) now drifts the party + runs their orders (the off-screen half of PL-M4-08), runs the jobs, feeds the residents, and **decays the shelter's barricades** — halved under a watchtower, held by a posted watch. The **fridge** spoils stash `item.food-fresh` to `item.food-spoiled` only when the grid is failing **and** neither a kitchen nor a running generator keeps it cold; the **generator** burns fuel to hold `world.powerGrid` up (which also keeps the fridge cold and the T51 carried-food clock at its slow rate).
- **Content — a new schema-validated type.** `content/schemas/job.schema.json` + **six jobs** take the gate **11 → 12 types** (5 new room recipes also ship under the existing recipe type).

## Verification

- **Every prior golden is byte-identical — proven cross-tree against the pre-T52 baseline.** A scripted deterministic run registering the full pre-T52 content set (regions/nodes/npcs/encounters/radio/recipes, **no jobs**) — 60 turns, including two off-screen `advanceWorld` jumps — was run on both the pristine pre-edit engine and the edited engine across **12 seeds**; the saves were **byte-for-byte equal**. Because T52 adds **no save-schema field** on a pool-less run (assignment is a flag that only ever appears once a job is assigned, which requires a job pool), **no normalization was needed** — a stronger guarantee than T51's. The adversarial engineering subagent independently reproduced this with a different policy/seeds (638 compared steps, 44 off-screen jumps with a base established, 0 mismatch). **No new lootable item** and **no loot-table growth** — jobs move only existing items between the stash and the world, so the `floor(f·len)` hazard T50/T51 had to gate simply never arises.
- **Determinism & no new RNG.** Every job is a pure deterministic conversion; `tickShelterOps` draws no stream. Same `(state, hours)` ⇒ byte-identical result. A render (`sceneOf → jobLine`, `jobChoices`, `buildableJobs`) advances nothing.
- **Save-losslessness.** A v10 run round-trips deep-equal after an assign, a produced cycle, a fed resident, a spoiled stash stack, a generator burn, and a watch upkeep; `meta.version` stays 10.
- **No stash underflow, no double-decay.** A stalled job leaves the stash non-negative; `powerGrid`/`barricades` are `clamp0to100`. Off-screen barricade decay runs **only** in `advanceWorld`; the on-screen stage-6 decay is untouched (verified: an on-screen rest decays by exactly the hours; a watchtower changes only the off-screen path).
- **Legibility (NFR-ACC-01 / FR-UI-02).** Job choice labels, the `jobLine` "daily report", and the `peopleLine` job read are all words — a worker's name, the job's world-effect, the base's doings ("it puts by fresh food; the cache has kept your people fed; the wall is not what it was"). No count, rate, or integrity number ever surfaces.

## Adversarial audit → fixes applied

Two subagents reviewed in parallel — an engineering lens (determinism / save / **byte-identity of every prior golden** / loot non-shift / off-screen-tick edges / forged-assignment edges) and a design lens (FR-SHL-03/04 fidelity / no-number-leak / voice / "the loops actually close"). The engineering lens **proved** the byte-identity guarantee (638-step cross-tree diff) and confirmed purity, save discipline, no-underflow, and no-double-decay as sound. Fixes applied:

- **HIGH (design) — the flagship daily report read "it puts by *food fresh*."** `item.food-fresh` de-hyphenated to the wrong adjective order in the marquee "the base ran while you were gone" line. Fixed: `jobs.ts` now spells out the compound-adjective foods (`item.food-fresh` → "fresh food", `item.food-spoiled` → "spoiled food") — the report reads grammatically.
- **MEDIUM (design) — a double period on every room build** ("something green can grow**.**. The room is yours now."): T51's `economyLine` appended `. The room…` after a `worldEffect` that already ends in a period. Fixed: strip the trailing period before appending (also fixes T51's workshop/medical builds).
- **MEDIUM (design) — the promised `job.watch` / upkeep shape was cut.** The plan promised six jobs across four shapes including a watchtower job; the first cut shipped five jobs / three shapes with the watchtower a passive only. Fixed: shipped **`job.watch`** (the `upkeepsBarricades` shape) — the watchtower is now postable, a resident actively holds the wall, and the "upkeep" shape the plan named exists.
- **MEDIUM (design) — off-screen wall decay was silent.** GDD XI wants "what happened at the walls" in the daily report, but `offscreenShelterUpkeep` lowered barricades with no beat. Fixed: a material off-screen loss now records a `shelter.weakened` beat, and `jobLine` surfaces "the barricades have taken the weather and the days — the wall is not what it was."
- **LOW (eng) — the generator burned fuel at a full grid for zero gain, with no feedback.** Fixed: a hold-power/upkeep job stalls when there's no headroom, before consuming — fuel is only spent when it can actually hold the grid up.
- **LOW (design) — the people line read an imperative mid-sentence** ("Ruth is with you — tend the garden…"). Fixed: "…— set to tend the garden for the base."
- **ROBUSTNESS (self-found) — `jobLine` could shadow the report with a stale assign beat.** `advanceWorld` reuses the acting turn's number, so a same-turn assign + off-screen tick shared a turn; the assign line early-returned over the production/weakened report. Fixed: `jobLine` now collects the turn's beats and prefers the "what happened" report over a bare assign/clear acknowledgement.

## Parking lot / deferrals

- **PL-M4-35 — full off-screen people-sim (PL-M3-02 / PL-M4-08 remainder).** T52 simulates the base's *own* residents and jobs off-screen; survivors elsewhere in the city still don't drift or move off-screen (kept out of `advanceWorld` deliberately, so `tickNpcs` is on-turn only). That, and desertion/betrayal & inter-NPC bonds, is the **T53** factions block. The shelter half is what T52 lands.
- **PL-M4-36 — sub-cycle granularity (PL-M2-04).** A job produces `trunc(hours / hoursPerCycle)` per tick (the scavenge idiom), so a short turn under one cycle banks nothing and a single long `advanceWorld` differs from many short ones by the truncated remainder (and a long single advance can starve a resident the feed pass can't top up in one shot). Hour-by-hour off-screen stepping is the deferred PL-M2-04; T52 matches the existing scavenge/guard model rather than adding a second one.
- **PL-M4-37 — job/room dials untuned (M5 balance T59/T60).** `DEFAULT_HOURS_PER_CYCLE 6`, per-job produce/consume quantities, `GEN_POWER_PER_CYCLE 20`, `WATCH_UPKEEP_PER_CYCLE 6`, `RESIDENT_FEED_AT 55` / `RESIDENT_FEED_RELIEF 40`, `STASH_SPOIL_HOURS 12`, the `WATCHTOWER_DECAY_DIVISOR 2`, and the room-recipe costs are first-pass identity values, not balanced against a real cross-city run.
- **PL-M4-38 — morale & friendships from shared labour (FR-NPC-07) + trading the surplus (FR-ECO-08).** Residents working a base together is the natural seat of inter-NPC bonds and shelter morale, and a self-sustaining base *produces* surplus to trade — both need the **T53** relationship/faction model; T52 ships the production loop, not the social one.
- **PL-M4-39 — a base worth raiding, but not yet raided live (PL-M3-06/08).** Jobs and a stocked cache make the base a real target, but the live night-siege that attacks it is still the deferred contested-world beat.
