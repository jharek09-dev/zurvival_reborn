# QA Review — M4 Part 7 (T51): The economy — resource loops, spoilage, crafting, repairs

**Scope:** T51 — FR-ECO-04 (four resource loops, each with a drain *and* a sink), FR-ECO-05 (food spoilage, faster after power loss; water purification), FR-ECO-06 (crafting — medical/weapon/shelter/survival — gated by blueprints, components, and rooms), FR-ECO-07 (repairs keep artifacts alive; repair-over-replace). GDD Part X ("Inventory, Crafting, Loot & Economy") + Part XI (rooms); the M4 wireframe **SCR-10** (Workshop — Crafting & Repair).
**Build verified (clean cloud sandbox):** engine **497** · content-loader **9** · harness **99** · typecheck clean ×3 · schema gate **146 entries / 11 types** · malformed rejected · empty-turn smoke exit 0.
**Save schema:** **one forward-only rung, v9 → v10** (`migrateV9toV10`). The player gains `economy: {blueprints, freshness}` and every node gains `rooms: []`; both seed at their inert empties, so a migrated pre-economy save is byte-identical. Mirrors the routes/stash/npcs "add an empty field, bump" rungs.
**Owner call worth flagging:** one content-driven system closes all four loops rather than four bolted-on meters — every craft/repair/purify debits real components + **time**, spoilage is the passive body sink, durability wear + repair is the safety sink, and fuel-burn in crafting/purification is the power sink. The whole system is **dark without a recipe pool**, so every prior run is byte-identical (proven against the pre-T51 baseline, below).

## What shipped

A new `sim/economy.ts` interprets `content/recipes/*.json` generically (the T47/T50 idiom — no per-recipe branching), over a transient `graph.recipes` pool threaded like the node graph. The seam mirrors radio's `Choices / isAction / resolve / Line`, wired into the same four sites in `coreActions.ts`. The whole system is **gated on an active recipe pool** and (for the bench verbs) on standing in your own shelter with the parts, so it's inert otherwise.

- **FR-ECO-04 — four loops, each with a real sink.** The pre-part audit found body & health had honest sinks but **safety durability was declared and never spent** and **power had no sink at all**. T51 closes both: **body** — food spoilage (the passive sink) plus fresh food now *eatable* (better food, a reason to eat it before it rots); **safety** — an equipped durability artifact wears with each melee strike and is restored only by repair; **health** — crafted bandages / antiseptic-based antibiotics; **power** — boiling water and running the bench debit `item.fuel`. Every recipe/verb debits a real resource; nothing is free, time included.
- **FR-ECO-05 — spoilage + purification.** `item.food-fresh` (findable only via economy-active loot) ages on a single `economy.freshness` clock, decremented in stage 4 by `1 × (world.powerGrid < POWER_SPOIL_AT ? POWER_SPOIL_MULT : 1)` — "faster once the grid fails" falls out of a signal the world already drifts (T27), no new bookkeeping. At `0` the stack becomes `item.food-spoiled` (a thin, desperate meal — a real loss, not a dead item). Canned food is shelf-stable and never spoils, so no prior run is touched. `item.water-dirty` can never be drunk directly; a `purify` recipe (boil = fuel, or a charcoal-and-cloth filter) is the only path to safe `item.water`.
- **FR-ECO-06 — crafting gated by blueprints, components, rooms.** A `RecipeDef` folds all three gates into one schema: a **room** is built by a shelter recipe (`installsRoom`) and required by another (`room`); a **blueprint** is learned into `player.economy.blueprints` (found as `item.blueprint.*`, studied) and required by a recipe (`blueprint`); **components** are the `inputs`. The workshop listing states a **missing part** (every shortfall, amber) instead of hiding a shown recipe — SCR-10's "says exactly what it needs."
- **FR-ECO-07 — repair-over-replace.** A `repair` recipe restores a carried durability artifact and **appends a provenance line to its `metadata`** — the instance id never changes, and the prose reads as the artifact's story growing ("its third repair … it carries the marks now, and it holds"), never a stat bump.
- **Content — a new schema-validated type.** `content/schemas/recipe.schema.json` + **11 recipes** across all six categories (medical/weapon/shelter/survival/repair/purify) take the gate **10 → 11 types**.

## Verification

- **Every prior golden is byte-identical — proven against the pre-T51 baseline.** An adversarial subagent extracted the pristine pre-edit tree and ran identical deterministic scripted runs (heavy searching across every loot-kind, plus combat) on both builds across **13 seeds**; after normalizing away the three legitimately-added fields (`player.economy`, `node.rooms`, `meta.version`), the saves were **byte-for-byte equal** — no loot draw, inventory item, region-loot value, or rng-stream state differed. The economy loot items are appended to the tables **only when the economy is active** (the `includeRadio` discipline — a `floor(f·len)` pick shifts every index when a table grows), so an economy-less run draws exactly as before. The `eat`-path change was separately re-verified byte-identical to the baseline across 5 eat/drink-heavy seeds (canned-food packs eat exactly as before).
- **Determinism & no new RNG.** Crafting/repair/purify/study are pure deterministic conversions — the economy draws **no** stream. Spoilage is a time-based countdown. A render (`sceneOf → economyLine`, `workshopListing`, `craftable`) advances nothing and is resume-safe.
- **Save-losslessness.** A v10 run round-trips deep-equal after a craft, a metadata-growing repair (nested `JsonValue`), a minted artifact in `state.items`, a purify, a study, and a spoil.
- **Integer discipline.** Durability, freshness, and all quantities are integer under `trunc`/`clamp0to100`.
- **Legibility (NFR-ACC-01 / FR-UI-02).** Recipe rows are all words: a label, a world-effect ("Boards the east window"), and a cost clause of counts + hours + the word "loud" ("2 cloth · 1h"). Repair narration uses ordinal *words*. No durability/percent/rarity number ever surfaces.
- **No no-op resolved turn.** Every economy verb costs ≥ 1h and changes state (inventory/items/rooms/blueprints + a beat), so none can be a no-consequence turn (FR-CORE-04).

## Adversarial audit → fixes applied

Two subagents reviewed in parallel — an engineering lens (determinism / save / **byte-identity of every prior golden** / loot-draw non-shift / forged-action edge cases) and a design lens (SCR-10 fidelity / no-number-leak / FR-ECO coverage / "always a little short" fairness / voice). The engineering lens **proved** the byte-identity guarantee (13-seed baseline diff) and confirmed purity, save discipline, and integer discipline as sound. Fixes applied:

- **BLOCKER (design) — the body loop was only nominally closed.** `item.food-fresh` was findable and spoilable but **could never be eaten** (`eat`/`canEat` were hardcoded to canned food), so spoilage drained a valueless item and the "loss you feel" prose described a loss that couldn't happen. Fixed: `eat` now reaches for the most perishable food first (fresh → canned → spoiled), each with its own relief — fresh is *better* food (a real "eat it now vs hoard the shelf-stable can" decision, the germ of "the last can"), spoiled a thin desperate meal. Canned-food behaviour is unchanged, so the golden path stays byte-identical.
- **MEDIUM (eng) — a forged `study` action could learn a blueprint for free** (the study dispatch didn't re-validate like craft/repair/purify). Fixed: `study` now requires the economy active AND the schematic actually carried; a forged action is inert (and dark without a pool).
- **MEDIUM (eng) — an already-built room recipe stayed craftable and re-crafting silently burned the inputs** (reachable in honest play). Fixed: `craftable` now rejects an `installsRoom` recipe whose room already exists, so the bench stops offering it.
- **LOW (eng) — a forged repair aimed at a full-durability artifact wasted inputs and logged a bogus provenance line.** Fixed: `repair` now requires the target below full durability.
- **LOW (eng) — the minted-artifact id was turn-stamped only** (a forged 0-cost double-mint could collide). Fixed: the id now appends a numeric backstop if the base id is taken.
- **SHOULD-FIX (design) — the charcoal-filter purify narrated "boil."** Fixed: `economyLine` now branches on the recipe (boil vs filter).
- **SHOULD-FIX (design) — three crafted outputs (molotov / torch / warm-clothing) had no consumers and their prose over-promised effects** (a thrown-weapon / light / cold system doesn't exist yet). Fixed: the `worldEffect` now describes the **made object** rather than a mechanic ("A fuel-filled bottle with a cloth wick …"), and the stat-word "damage" is gone; their effect systems are parked (PL-M4-30).
- **SHOULD-FIX (design) — the missing-part surface reported only the *first* shortfall** and an authored `missingHint` field was dead. Fixed: `workshopListing.missing` now returns **every** shortfall; the dead field is removed.

## Parking lot / deferrals

- **PL-M4-29 — base / stash spoilage (GDD X "the fridge").** T51 spoils only the *carried* fresh stack; food banked in `player.stash` is abstracted as preserved. The community fridge that fails with the grid, per-stash spoilage, and a fuel-burning generator that *holds* `powerGrid` up belong with **T52** (shelter depth — jobs, storage, the daily report).
- **PL-M4-30 — crafted outputs awaiting their effect systems.** `item.molotov` (thrown-weapon / area-fire), `item.torch` (light / night-danger), and `item.warm-clothing` (cold / weather-exposure) are craftable and carryable but inert until those systems land (combat throw, a light/exposure model). Their prose was softened so nothing over-promises; hold or wire them when the systems ship. Likewise the GDD-X **shelter crafts** (barricades / reinforcements / alarms) beyond room-installs — the fortify system already does barricades (T38); a "reinforced plank" recipe is a seam.
- **PL-M4-31 — economy dials are untuned (M5 balance T59/T60).** `FRESH_SHELF_LIFE 48`, `POWER_SPOIL_AT 40` / `POWER_SPOIL_MULT 2`, recipe costs/times, `WEAPON_WEAR 2`, repair `+40`, and the component/blueprint loot rates are first-pass. Notes for the pass: repair is very cheap (near-free maintenance softens the durability sink), and 48h shelf life means fresh food is rarely lost unless the grid is actually failing. The system installs the sinks that *can* produce "the last can"; it tunes none.
- **PL-M4-32 — spoiled-food sickness cost + per-food eat choice.** Spoiled food is eatable at low relief with no health cost (a soft loss); a real infection/sickness cost on the desperate meal, and letting the player *choose which* food to eat (rather than fresh-first), are enhancements for the balance/depth pass. Also: one shared `freshness` clock per fresh stack (late-added fresh food inherits the old countdown), `repairTarget` picks the first worn artifact, and a mint auto-equips silently — all acceptable MVP abstractions.
- **PL-M4-33 — repair-provenance render.** `metadata.repairs` stores raw `{from,to}` durability integers (internal, unrendered). Before any inventory screen prints the ledger, add a durability-band-to-words helper so the exact numbers don't leak (FR-UI-02). The legendary firefighter's-axe seed (SCR-10's repair example) rides on `item.tool-reinforced` for now; the full legendary set is post-gate.
- **PL-M4-34 — trading + hidden loot + NPC hints (FR-ECO-08/09).** Scarcity/reputation-priced exchange needs factions (T53); curiosity caches + a survivor tip that resolves to real loot is a T54 depth-screen / radio-rumor beat. Neither ships here.
